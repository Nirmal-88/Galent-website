For much of the early 2000s, India's Global Capability Centers (GCCs) were dismissed as back-office operations, low-cost extensions of western corporations doing grunt work at scale.

That narrative has flipped.

India's GCCs have exploded into powerhouse innovation hubs – home to global product engineering teams, creators of AI copilots for frontline operations, and architects of cloud-native platforms – fueling the world's AI revolution.

## What is driving this evolution?

It's not just cost savings anymore – it's capability, increasingly, AI capability; powered by India's evolving GCC landscape and resilient capability.

This blog explores this transformation of GCCs in India, it examines the rise of GenAI and Agentic AI as key accelerants, and unpacks how ecosystems, talent, and solutions are shaping India's emergence as the world's AI co-creation capital.

## India's GCC Advantage: Scale, Sophistication, and Strategic Value

Indian GCCs are now vital nodes in global innovation networks.

Over 185 centers have launched dedicated AI/ML Centers of Excellence (CoEs), with more than 30% focused in software and internet verticals. And it's not just the majors anymore – a new generation of product-first, AI-native GCCs are emerging from day one.

## What's driving this transformation?

India's GCC evolution is being propelled by a broader enterprise shift towards scaling global capabilities, accelerating time-to-market, and building resilient, future-ready delivery models. While AI is a key accelerant, the real driver is strategic intent: companies are using GCCs to expand global footprint, deepen engineering muscle, and create integrated hubs that go beyond support to deliver end-to-end impact.

Today's GCCs are growth engines enabling everything from product innovation to CX transformation, all while ensuring operational scale, agility, and global consistency.

## GCC Transformation: The Shift from Offshoring to Orchestrating Change

Enterprises' views of India have shifted dramatically over the decades – from low-cost offshoring centers to a hub for captive IT and R&D units, to now, full-spectrum capability centers driving strategic transformation.

This progression marks a structural shift in how global companies view India – not just as a cost arbitrage play, but as a core value creation engine.

## Resilience in an Uncertain World

Even amid the dual disruptions of AI acceleration and global macroeconomic and geopolitical instability, the growth of India's GCC ecosystem has not only persisted – but it has also gained momentum.

This resilience signals something deeper: a fundamental mindset shift in the boardrooms of global enterprises. More than ever, leaders are looking to de-risk operations, access AI-ready talent, and unlock long-term innovation leverage – and India delivers on all three.

## From Execution to Innovation Leadership

India's tech talent is evolving fast – over 2 million engineers graduate annually, many specializing in AI, data science, and automation. Roles like Quantum Architects, AI Ethics Officers, and ML Security Experts are increasingly becoming common, signaling a shift from execution roles to innovation leadership.

* It's Not Just About Cost Anymore. It's Capability. AI Capability.
* It's not just about saving money. It's about building the capability stack required to thrive in the AI era.
* Nearly 185 GCCs have established dedicated AI/ML Centers of Excellence.
* Over 30% are concentrated in the software and internet sectors.

India's workforce includes 2M+ engineers graduating annually, many with specialization in AI, data science, and automation.

And the value is starting to show up in national economic indicators. According to India's Economic Survey 2024, GCCs are projected to contribute 3.5% to India's GDP by 2030, generating $121 billion in revenue.

## The Gen AI Inflection Point

If traditional AI marked a turning point for enterprise transformation, Gen AI is the accelerant – reshaping how GCCs deliver innovation, speed, and scale.

Across India's GCC landscape, Gen AI adoption has moved beyond pilots and prototypes. It's now a strategic imperative.

* 70% of GCCs are actively implementing Gen AI use cases
* 78% are investing in workforce upskilling
* 37% have already deployed Gen AI in core functions like software engineering, analytics, and customer experience

(Source: EY India GCC Pulse Survey 2024)

Gen AI is deeply embedded in enhancing CI/CD pipelines, automating content, and boosting developer productivity with AI copilots. The rise of low-code/no-code GenAI tools is equally transformative, empowering business users to build apps and workflows without writing code.

GCCs aren't just scaling GenAI – they're building CoEs, setting governance models, and turning teams into innovation engines.

## The result?

A redefined role for India's GCCs – from cost-effective delivery centers to AI-native value engines that drive enterprise reinvention.

Indian GCCs are no longer just executing – they're inventing, innovating, and leading global enterprises' digital transformations. As the world's GCC capital, India is rewriting the playbook on what these centers can achieve.

As digital transformation accelerates, mid to large-sized enterprises are increasingly looking to replicate this model – to build their own centers of excellence, tap into India's deep talent pool, and drive innovation closer to global markets. However, they often face a steep learning curve: unfamiliar regulations, talent strategy gaps, and the complexity of setting up operations in a new geography.

## The Next Frontier: Agentic AI and Autonomous GCCs

We've entered the era of agentic AI – where AI doesn't just support work. It does the work. According to Gartner, "33% of enterprise software will include agentic features by 2028."

## Why is India Uniquely Positioned for the Agentic Leap?

India's GCCs have rapidly matured from AI adopters to AI builders – tech penetration in AI/ML has jumped from 65% in 2019 to 86% in 2024. With a surge of agentic AI startups, academic backing, and policy momentum, India is emerging as the epicenter of next-gen enterprise automation.

By 2028, a third of enterprise software will feature agentic capabilities and Indian GCCs will be leading their development. From autonomous design systems to self-healing ops agents, these centers are evolving into AI-native ecosystems that automate full workflows, not just tasks.

From cost centers to cognitive command centers – India's GCCs are becoming autonomous by design. Cities like Chennai and Pune are already pioneering this shift – turning India's GCCs into cognitive command hubs for the agentic era.
