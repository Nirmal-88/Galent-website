## Galent's Strategic Role Classification: A Precision Approach to Workforce Transformation

To harness GenAI's full potential, organizations must rethink workforce segmentation at a granular level. Galent's Role Transformation Strategy deconstructs traditional IT roles into 'subroutines', enabling precision in AI deployment:

* **Automate** – low-complexity, high-repetition tasks (e.g., password resets).
* **Augment** – AI-assisted workflows (e.g., AI-powered code reviews, ticket triaging).
* **Preserve** – high-value, human-driven tasks requiring complex problem-solving.

Roles are classified based on two critical dimensions:

* **Exposure** – The likelihood of automation replacing tasks.
* **Friction** – The ease of transitioning workers into new roles with existing skills.

For example:

DevOps Engineers have high exposure to automation, requiring reskilling into AI-integrated workflows.

Hardware Engineers face high friction, necessitating significant retraining due to specialized, hands-on expertise.

By applying this structured approach, enterprises can systematically align AI adoption with workforce transformation, optimizing resource allocation while safeguarding human ingenuity.

## The 4 Archetypes of GenAI Workforce Integration

The 4 Archetypes for Gen-AI Integration highlight how different tasks within the workforce can be reimagined through automation, augmentation, and optimization, enabling organizations to enhance efficiency while empowering employees to focus on higher-value work.

* **Commoditized:** Routine tasks that require minimal expertise.
* **Automated:** Repetitive, rule-based, and fully automatable tasks.
* **Augmented:** AI-driven insights enhance human decision-making.
* **Human-Centric:** Tasks requiring creativity, empathy, and risk management.

By applying this modular approach, organizations can maximize the value of their workforce while integrating AI solutions in a way that drives meaningful business transformation
