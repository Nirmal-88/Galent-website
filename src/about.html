Most AI-services firms exist on one side of the gap or the other. Either elegant model work that never reaches production, or muscular delivery that hasn't been rebuilt for AI. Both sides leave the gap unaddressed.

02 · The journey

How we got here.

Galent did not start as an AI company. It started as a delivery company that watched AI re-write what delivery means.

2014 — THE SIGNAL

The pyramid begins to bend

Digital transformation generated a decade of headcount-led growth in tech services. Staffing margins rose; services margins quietly declined. The fracture began.

2022 — THE INFLECTION

Generative AI lands in production

LLMs cross the line from research to deployment. Industry response is to bolt copilots onto unchanged workflows. The fracture widens.

2024 — GALENT FOUNDED

An AI-native engineering company, by design

Founded to operate on the other side of the fracture. Not a services firm retro-fitting AI. A delivery model engineered around AI from the first line of code.

2025 — GALENTAI · v1

The platform goes live

NeuroQL, RCM, Knowledge Graph, and Context Graph ship as a coherent system. First enterprise programmes move to GalentAI-orchestrated delivery.

2026 — TODAY

Forward-Deployed Engineering at scale

Embedded FDEs run programmes inside enterprise teams across BFSI, healthcare, manufacturing, and tech. Delivery velocity at 3–4× conventional, with quality and compliance enforced by design.

03 · BELIEFS
What we refuse to compromise.
01

Outcomes, not hours.

Programmes are scoped by what they ship. Time-and-materials engineering rewards drift; we don't operate that way.

02

AI-native, not AI-assisted.

A copilot bolted onto a legacy SDLC is a productivity tool. AI-native delivery is a different category — and the only one that compounds.

03

Context is the asset.

Code is renewable. Context — the why, the constraints, the rationale — is what compounds. We engineer for context persistence.

04

Quality at generation, not after.

Quality reviews are a tax on missed information upstream. We move the bar to where work is made, not where it is judged.

05

Embed, don't ship over the wall.

FDEs sit inside your team. Translation cost approaches zero; ownership stays where the outcome is created.

06

Leave teams faster, not dependent.

Lock-in is a failure mode, not a business model. When we leave, your team is faster, your platform is yours, your context stays.
